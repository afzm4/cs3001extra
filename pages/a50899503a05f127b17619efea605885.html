Student-run detective noir comedy keeps you guessing \xe2\x80\x98til the end

\n
Posted by Delia Croessmann
On September 18, 2018
\n
\n

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

Photo courtesy of Wikimedia Commons

\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t \n \n
\n\n\n
\n \n\n \n \n

Missouri S&T’s collegiate theater society presents its 2018 play this month \xe2\x80\x93 a comedic parody of classic detective murder mystery films.

\n

Performances of \xe2\x80\x9cKill Me, Deadly\xe2\x80\x9d will be held at 7 p.m. Thursday, Sept. 27, Friday, Sept. 28, and Saturday, Sept. 29, and at 2 p.m. Sunday, Sept. 30 at the Black Box Theatre in Room 143 Castleman Hall, located at the corner of 10th and Main streets in Rolla.

\n

Tickets are $5 for Missouri S&T students and $7 for the general public. Tickets may be reserved in advance by calling 573-341-4185 or emailing Renee Williams at williamsmelr@mst.edu. Tickets may also be purchased at the door. The show\xe2\x80\x99s runtime is two hours with a 15-minute intermission.

\n

Students Brandon Marolt, playing detective Charlie Nickels, and Raelynn Twohy, playing his secretary Ida, rehearse a scene for “Kill Me, Deadly.”

\n

The Missouri S&T production is entirely student run and is presented by the university\xe2\x80\x99s chapter of Alpha Psi Omega, a national theater honor society. Nineteen students from S&T \xe2\x80\x93 mostly from engineering and computing disciplines \xe2\x80\x93 comprise the cast of characters.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cKill Me, Deadly\xe2\x80\x9d was written by Bill Robens in 2009 and made into a neo-noir comedy film in 2015. The plot revolves around Detective Charlie Nickels\xe2\x80\x99 intense sleuthing to solve the mystery of his client\xe2\x80\x99s murder.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cWe\xe2\x80\x99re lucky to have an incredibly talented cast and technical crew,\xe2\x80\x9d says Hannah Ramsey-Standage, a senior in English from Rolla, Missouri, and co-director of the play. \xe2\x80\x9cThe text of the play pokes a little bit of fun at the classic detective, film noir genre while creating a fun show for audiences to enjoy. There\xe2\x80\x99s lots of intrigue and a constantly changing plot as new clues appear.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Co-director Olivia Holdman, a senior in geology and geophysics from Festus, Missouri, and president of S&T\xe2\x80\x99s Alpha Psi Omega chapter, is also technical director for a crew of 19 additional students. \xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s great to have the opportunity to work with so many people on this production, from familiar faces to brand new ones,\xe2\x80\x9d says Holdman. \xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s so wonderful seeing that our organization will continue to grow for the foreseeable future.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Alpha Psi Omega is dedicated to the growth and development of Missouri S&T\xe2\x80\x99s theater department and all related groups. As a\xc2\xa0service organization, it strives to help its members achieve their personal goals within the arts. Proceeds from \xe2\x80\x9cKill Me, Deadly\xe2\x80\x9d will be used to fund Alpha Psi Omega\xe2\x80\x99s organizational activities for the year.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cKill Me, Deadly\xe2\x80\x9d contains adult themes and language and is not recommended for children under the age of 13.
